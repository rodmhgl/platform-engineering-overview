# How to Communicate with Executives

## What Executives Care About

Executives are primarily concerned with:

- **Business value**
- **Strategic alignment**
- **Organization's success and financial health**

## Communication Strategies

### Frame Around Key Business Objectives

#### Reducing Time to Market

Explain how standardized, automated processes (golden paths) accelerate the software delivery lifecycle, enabling faster release of products and featuresâ€”directly linked to **competitive advantage**.

#### Cost Efficiency and ROI

Clearly articulate how the MVP contributes to:

- **Reduced operational overhead** through standardization
- **Potential automation** of tasks
- **Lowering infrastructure costs** through optimized resource utilization

Present a clear **ROI calculation** projecting tangible cost savings and productivity gains.

#### Risk Management and Compliance

Explain how the MVP incorporates **security and compliance from the outset**, potentially through secure development workflows. Highlight how this can **mitigate costly security breaches** and associated penalties.

#### Talent Retention

Connect the platform to **improved developer experience**, reducing frustration with manual processes and leading to:

- Increased job satisfaction
- Potentially lower attrition rates

### Support with Data and External Validation

- Use **metrics gathered during MVP phase**
- Leverage **external confirmation** such as Gartner reports or industry case studies

### Align with Broader Strategic Goals

Position the platform initiative as a key enabler for larger organizational objectives such as **digital transformation or infrastructure modernisation**.

## Presentation Style

- Use **clear business language**
- Support with **data and visualisations**
- Focus on **long-term impact and strategic benefits**
- Avoid getting bogged down in technical details
